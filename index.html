01 THE ORIGIN STORY October 19, 2016

LOCAL DESIGNER
BREAKS THE
INTERNET.

New Orleans, LA  ·  October 19, 2016  ·  11:47 PM

On October 19, 2016, designer Amanda Brinkman created the Nasty Woman shirt within minutes of Trump calling Clinton a "nasty woman" during the third presidential debate. By the next morning, the design had gone globally viral — one of the fastest-spreading pieces of protest merchandise in political history.

Covered within days by Reuters, The Washington Post, People, and dozens of international outlets, the shirt became a symbol of the resistance and ignited a cultural conversation that defined the years that followed.

Rather than capitalize on viral momentum with cheap production, Brinkman built something deliberate: a curated platform for independent feminist designers, an ethical supply chain, and a giving model built into every product decision.

06 THE SHOUT Weekly Newsletter

Class Publication

The
Shout.

Pop culture. Hot takes. Politics. What we're reading, watching, buying, and refusing to shut up about.

100K

Subscribers — Word of Mouth Only

What it was

A bi-weekly newsletter written in a frank, comedic voice — Shrill Society's co-founders sharing what they were reading, watching, thinking about, listening to, buying, and attending. No sponsored content. No brand voice. Just opinions.

How it grew

No promotion. No paid acquisition. The Shout grew entirely through word-of-mouth, with readers forwarding it to like-minded friends. It reached 100,000 subscribers organically — a direct reflection of the brand's cultural credibility.

★ Most Likely to Build Something That Matters

Amanda
Brinkman

Senior Brand & Marketing Leader  ·  Founder, Shrill Society

Amanda Brinkman is a senior brand and marketing leader who builds positioning, narrative, and growth engines for consumer and B2B organizations operating at cultural and commercial scale — from viral consumer moments to enterprise B2B programs. She leads integrated go-to-market efforts that sharpen brand clarity, strengthen equity, and translate storytelling into measurable business impact.

Her client and partner roster includes Red Bull, Ellevest, She Should Run, Blume, Clean Plates, Maker's Mark, Refinery29, Girlboss, and Urban Outfitters, with experience across corporate, agency, and entrepreneurial environments.

Brinkman founded Shrill Society in 2016 after designing the Nasty Woman shirt, which became a mainstream cultural phenomenon. Under her leadership, the brand expanded into licensed products, global distribution, and a publishing partnership with Penguin Random House. Her work has been featured 300+ times in leading publications including The New York Times, Fortune, The Washington Post, The Cut, Teen Vogue, Allure, and Glamour.
